ABOUT ESFC

The proposed geophysical field camp will be held on August 2020 in El-Fayoum province, Egypt. This field camp will be in cooperation between 3 of the most important universities in Egypt, "SEG Al-Azhar, SEG Cairo, SEG Alexandria", with the Egyptian Geophysical Society (EGS) and the National Institute of Astronomical and Geophysical Research (NRIAG). Egypt seg field camp " ESFC" will be the first geophysical camp not only in Egypt but also in the Middle East and the Arab world as we mentioned before that it's held in only 3 countries, and Egypt will be the fourth. The aim of this camp is not only to work practically on tools, but we will work on soft skills,.

42 Participants

Since we are looking to make the most of this camp, the field will be restricted to geophysics students only. So our scope is: Special-Geophysics students, Double-major geophysics students, and Petroleum Geology students.

7 Programs

This field camp contains many programs, not just technical programs, but much more. The seven programs are: Acquisition, processing, interpretation, astronomical observation, tourism, games, and science show.

5 Speakers

To make this field camp one of the greatest experiences for the participants, we will have 5 speeches, Technical speech, motivational speech, soft skills speech, online learning speech, and career development speech.

Do you have Questions?

If you have any questions you will find here some answers for popular questions.

What is SEG field camp?

The SEG Field Camp program is intended to advance the science of geophysics and support aspiring geoscientists by providing critical funding for projects that promote the professional development, student support, and youth outreach goals of the SEG.

Applicants from around the world are encouraged to apply. The Field Camp program provides grants ranging from $1,000-$20,000 annually per project (depending on available funding).

Where is will be ESFC In egypt?

In El Fayoum province, Egypt. EL- Fayoum is a city in Middle Egypt. Located 100 kilometers southwest of Cairo surrounded by desert from all directions except form the south-east direction where it is connected to the Nile Valley by a Canal called Bahr Youssef (Fig. 1).

It includes two areas declared as protected by the Egyptian Government: Lake Qarun and Wadi Rayan National Parks. El-Fayoum province is one of the largest depressions in the western desert of Egypt

What Our Project Goals and Objectives?

Increase the technical experience of the students in these fields by preparing sessions before the camp.
Develop the practical experience of the students in this field to increase their opportunity to work by the practical work on the camp.
Improve the Geophysics exposure and SEG reach in Egypt by the media coverage of the event.
Increase the participant's network in the field to mentor their effort and development by the project day after the camp.
Give the participant's some nontechnical experience like teamwork and presentation skills during all the events.
Increase the students' Knowledge in the updates and advanced geophysics techniques by the workshops and speeches in the event.
Develop the work experiences of the winning team by giving them internships in a petroleum company.
